Codara — PFE Methodology

DSR + Agile Hybrid

Why Design Science Research drives the thesis framing, and why two-week sprints keep the engineering honest — a visual walkthrough.

01 — Choosing a methodologyThree candidates, one winner

Before committing to a framework, three standard approaches were evaluated against the dual nature of this project: it is simultaneously a research contribution (novel pipeline architecture, formal verification) and a delivered software product.

🏃
Pure Scrum
Iterative delivery, product-owner driven, 2-week sprints
  • + Fast delivery cycles, works well for product teams
  • + Backlog + velocity metrics are concrete and measurable
  • − No research framing — "why does this matter?" is out-of-scope
  • − No artefact evaluation theory (no rigour model)
  • − Research contribution cannot be expressed as a user story
  • − Knowledge contribution is never validated, only shipped
✗ Rejected — adequate for products, insufficient for thesis research
📊
CRISP-DM
Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining (Wirth & Hipp, 2000)
  • + Strong data understanding → modelling → evaluation loop
  • + Industry-recognised in analytics / ML contexts
  • ~ Pairs naturally with ML calibration and ablation studies
  • − Focused on predictive modelling, not system artefact construction
  • − No framework for building novel tools or engines
  • − No theory-building component — purely empirical
~ Partially adopted — CRISP-DM phases used internally within the DSR Evaluation phase
🔬
DSR + Agile
Hevner et al. 2004 + two-week sprint cadence
  • + Explicitly designed for IT artefact construction and evaluation
  • + 7 guidelines provide a publishable rigour checklist
  • + Research output = the artefact + its evaluation evidence
  • + Agile sprints manage the 14-week clock without losing research focus
  • + Hevner's environment ↔ artefact ↔ knowledge base triangle fits exactly
  • ~ Heavier to document — mitigated by CLAUDE.md + weekly logs
✓ Chosen — the only framework that validates both the artefact and the knowledge contribution
💡

The core argument

Codara is not just software — it is a claim about how LLM-driven migration pipelines should be built. That claim requires a research framework that demands evaluation evidence (DSR guideline 3) and situates the contribution in the IS knowledge base (guideline 7). Scrum ships features; DSR validates knowledge.

02 — The DSR frameworkHevner's 5-phase cycle

Design Science Research (Hevner et al., 2004; Peffers et al., 2007) structures research as artefact construction — a cycle that explicitly connects real-world problems to knowledge contributions.

1

Awareness of Problem

SAS migration costs €500K+ per enterprise; manual conversion scales at 200 LOC/day. No automated tool handles RETAIN, FIRST./LAST., cross-file macros at enterprise scale.

Week 1 — literature survey + Deloitte context
2

Suggestion

Propose: 8-node LangGraph pipeline with RAPTOR retrieval, Z3 formal verification, CDAIS adversarial testing, and three-tier LLM routing (Ollama → Azure → Groq).

Week 1–2 — architecture design
3

Development

Build the artefact across 13 two-week sprints. Each sprint adds one or more pipeline layers (L2-A through L4) and their test coverage. Redis checkpointing, DuckDB audit, LanceDB vector KB.

Week 2–13 — 7 sprint pairs
4

Evaluation

721-block gold corpus (boundary accuracy >90%), 10-block torture test, RAPTOR ablation (+36% hit-rate), SemantiCheck SCS_L3L4, Z3 proofs (3/10 blocks formally verified), 452 passing tests.

Week 12–14 — ablation, benchmarks, coverage
5

Conclusion

This thesis report + CDAIS paper + MIS paper. Documents what works, what the limits are, and what future work is needed (SemantiCheck L1/L2, multi-run variance, GPT-5.4 comparative results).

Week 14–15 — thesis writing + defence

03 — Hevner's 7 guidelinesApplied to Codara

Hevner et al. (2004) provide 7 guidelines that a DSR project must satisfy. Each maps to a concrete decision in Codara.

1

Design as an Artefact

Research must produce a viable artefact: a construct, model, method, or instantiation.

Codara is the artefact — a deployed instantiation (React UI + FastAPI + LangGraph pipeline) with quantified performance metrics.
2

Problem Relevance

The artefact addresses an important, unsolved business problem.

SAS-to-Python migration at Deloitte Tunisia (TMT practice): manual process, no enterprise-grade automated tool, €500K+ typical cost per project.
3

Design Evaluation

The artefact must be rigorously evaluated against the problem it claims to solve.

721-block gold corpus, torture test, Z3 formal proofs, CDAIS adversarial suite, SemantiCheck SCS, 452-test suite — all quantified with explicit metrics.
4

Research Contributions

The research must provide clear, verifiable contributions to the knowledge base.

Three contributions: (a) CDAIS coverage certificates, (b) MIS invariant selection, (c) RAPTOR+RAG pipeline architecture for code migration.
5

Research Rigor

Construction and evaluation apply rigorous methods from foundations and IS research.

Z3 SMT formal verification; ECE calibration (proper scoring rule); ablation study isolating RAPTOR contribution; single-annotator caveat explicitly acknowledged.
6

Design as a Search Process

Finding an adequate solution requires searching across the problem and solution space.

LLM routing explored Ollama → Azure → Groq → LocalModel. Boundary detection: lark grammar → regex → LLM fallback. Each iteration documented in weekly logs.
7

Communication of Research

Must be communicated to both technology-oriented and management-oriented audiences.

Thesis (academic) + CLAUDE.md / system docs (engineering) + React dashboard with full audit trail (Deloitte management audience).

04 — The Scrum problemWhy pure Scrum is insufficient

Scrum is excellent for product development. But a thesis is not a product — it is a knowledge claim with engineering evidence. Here is where pure Scrum breaks down for this project.

❌ What Scrum cannot handle

  • Research questions — "what principle generalises?" is not a user story
  • Knowledge contribution framing — sprints produce stories, not propositions
  • Evaluation rigour — definition of done ≠ empirical validity
  • Literature gap framing — product backlogs don't reference Hevner 2004
  • Formal verification — Z3 proofs don't fit a sprint review demo
  • Artefact evaluation vs acceptance — fundamentally different criteria

✓ What Agile sprints do provide

  • Time-boxed delivery — 14 weeks with 7 sprint pairs stays on track
  • Incremental complexity — L2-A before L2-B before L3 prevents big-bang risk
  • Velocity tracking — commit history + weekly logs (docs/logs/)
  • Adaptation — when Groq was rate-limited, Azure became primary mid-sprint
  • Testability cadence — 248 tests grew sprint-by-sprint alongside code
  • Risk mitigation — circuit breakers and fallbacks discovered in sprint retros
⚖️

The DSR–Agile contract

DSR owns the "why" and the "what": it defines the problem, the research questions, the evaluation criteria, and what counts as a knowledge contribution. Agile owns the "how" and the "when": it sequences the build, manages the 14-week clock, and provides the discipline to ship something that can actually be evaluated. Neither alone is sufficient. Together they make the research both rigorous and deliverable.

05 — The hybridHow DSR and Agile interlock

DSR provides the macro structure (5 phases over the thesis lifetime); Agile provides the micro cadence (2-week sprints within the Development phase). They run at different time scales and serve different purposes.

🔄

The feedback loop is the whole point

The arrow at the bottom of the diagram is what distinguishes DSR+Agile from just "iterative development". When Sprint 9 revealed RAPTOR gave +36% hit-rate (not the predicted +50%), that finding fed back into the DSR Suggestion phase — updating the research hypothesis and future work section — not just into the product backlog. The evaluation results are evidence for a knowledge claim, not just a retrospective.

07 — CRISP-DM inside DSRWhere data mining methodology fits

CRISP-DM was not rejected — it was embedded. Within the DSR Evaluation phase, the data science components (complexity calibration, ablation study, SemantiCheck scoring) follow CRISP-DM's empirical loop.

📦
Business Understanding
CRISP-DM phase 1
  • ✓ SAS migration cost, manual throughput, evaluation targets (>90% boundary accuracy, ≥70% E2E)
🗂️
Data Understanding + Preparation

  • ✓ 721-block gold corpus annotation, VTP corpus (130 pairs), complexity feature engineering
🧮
Modelling

  • ✓ ComplexityAgent ML calibration (logistic regression, ECE 0.061), RAPTOR GMM clustering, LanceDB IVF index
📏
Evaluation
CRISP-DM phase 5
  • ✓ Ablation study (RAPTOR vs flat index), SemantiCheck SCS_L3L4, Z3 proof rate, boundary benchmark accuracy
🧩

Three-level nesting: DSR ⊃ Agile ⊃ CRISP-DM

The complete picture: DSR is the outer framework (problem → suggestion → build → evaluate → communicate). Agile sprints execute the build phase in two-week increments. CRISP-DM governs the data science sub-tasks within the evaluation phase. Each level operates at the appropriate time scale and abstraction level.
